BUTCHER & CORREIA, together with Joana Bruno, develop visual design and costume for a performance exploring strategies of giving away and letting go. The project centres on a sustainable garment based on Grenne’s daily training clothes and a “soft scenography” of textiles and sound. The work treats dancer, material, and space as a resonant ecosystem where wool becomes a conductor of energy, and floor, mats, and fabrics amplify presence. Through ethical and material engagement—tracing supply, sustainability, and production constraints—the project exposes the compromises of local, natural production, linking performer and audience through shared frequency, attention, and tactility.

manifesto

Resonant Chamber — Every surface, sound, and silence carries energy. The work begins by tuning the space, not filling it.
Every Material is a Vehicle for a Story — Where the wool? Who the wool? What the wool? Why the wool?
Every Choice is a Compromise — Let the friction be visible. Let the contradictions remain.
Local ≠ Easy — To source near is to confront distance. To insist on proximity is to reveal loss.
Process is the Scene — The making is not preparation. It’s performance in slow motion.
Documentation is Choreography.
Friction is Data.
Logistics are Poetics.
Transparency Becomes Form — Reveal the scaffold: labor, production, legislation, supply.
Process as Performance / Performance as Process.
The Costume is a Membrane — Not decoration but instrument. Wool becomes skin, speaker, and witness.
Let the Costume Resist You — Let it shape your spine, tug your rhythm, interrupt your aesthetic.
Epilogue: A Call to Attunement — This work is not a product. It is a tuning practice. Let the work remain unfinished.
